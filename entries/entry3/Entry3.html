FRIED RICE

***

RICE
GIRL

Growing up in an Asian household, I have always been a rice girl. I love a bowl of good fried rice as it is arguably one of the most common dishes in my family. When away from home or my family, I am constantly craving some rice dishes ALL THE TIME (almost every day). So last week, my friend and I went to a very popular Thai restaurant, Pranakhon which has been on my list for a year as a little treat for ourselves after a long week of school.

As we pushed the door and entered the restaurant, we could already smell a strong aroma of Thai curry. We waited and got seated by the host, and I decided to order the crab fried rice on the menu, and out of my expectation, it tasted and smelt so good. The most noticeable smell was from its pungent garlic and onions and a subtle oceanic aroma from the crab meat and the sauce.

The whole dinning experience and environment brought up my memory from my Thailand trip before COVID hit. In December 2019, I joined a two-week school trip to Thailand, where we spent about ten days in Chang Mai and six days in Bangkok. During the trip, I had the opportunity to try different Thai dishes, and my favorite was the fried rice made by my homestay host in Chang Mai. I am sure it is some sort of a homemade recipe because I have never eaten fried rice that is so special (or maybe just because I am used to eating Chinese fried rice, not Thai fried rice).

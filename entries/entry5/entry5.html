Mimosa, also known as Acacia or Silver Wattle, produces flowers in various colors, including yellow, pink, white, and red. It is well-known for its beautiful yellow blossoms, the most common color of flower produced by this species. The yellow mimosa is native to Australia and commonly seen in forests, woodlands, and along watercourses. The mimosa flower has one of the most delicate, pleasant, and sweet scent, often described as honey-like.

STORY BEHIND

If I were to compare my childhood to a scent, mimosa, it would undoubtedly be the scent of mimosa, an extremely familiar aroma that always brings me back to my childhood. Since my grandparents lived very close to my family, we visited them almost every day back then. At their place, there were and still are some very strict rules we had to follow. For example, we have to wear slippers inside and remove them before stepping on the carpet. But the one rule that sticks out is handwashing. My grandma would catch us, who tried to skip it, saying, "You must wash your hands before you eat." I remember that she loved a particular hand soap that smelled like mimosa, which she kept repurchasing again and again. So now, whenever I come across that specific mimosa scent, I automatically think of my grandma and her favorite hand soap.

THE SMELL OF RAIN REMINDED ME OF MY HOME IN HONG KONG

PETRICHOR

A term defined as "the earthy scent produced when rain falls on dry soil" based on Wikipedia, which is probably my favourite smell. I always like rainy day. Not just because of the calmness and meditative sound of water drop hitting on the window, but because of the smell it brings along.

NEW YORK ON A RAINY DAY

The rain smells slightly different in New York, though, with a combination of smoke, marijuana, and an earthly scent. When I stepped out of my apartment building and was about to attend one of my evening classes, I noticed the smell of the fresh and clean air after a rain storm flushed everything away 30 minutes ago. I enjoyed this rare moment in New York's fresh air. But not long after, I scented cigarette smoke and marijuana odor, a familiar reminder that I was in New York.

Reminder of Home

In Hong Kong, where I grew up, we have a lot of rain during summer. I would say I spent almost half of my summer carrying an umbrella just in case it started to pour rain. Therefore, I have many memories associated with the smell of rain, most of which are delightful memories. My home in Hong Kong is on a hill. It is a small and quiet hill with a relatively low population. Because of that, there are tons of trees, plants, stray cats and dogs, and even wild animals like monkeys living on the hill (sometimes they even swim in the pool of our building). So you can imagine whenever it rains, there is always a strong earthy scent in the air. Now, whenever I encounter the smell of rain, it reminds me of the rainy days in Hong Kong when I would be sitting on a couch, listening to the sound of the rain, and enjoying this pleasant moment.

CAMPFIRE

The placemat from Muji has a distinguishable smell of burning-wood that resemble the smell of campfires in Temagami and everytime I set my table, I would recall such memory from the trip

Outdoor education

My high school has this little island up in Temagami in the northern part of Ontario, Canada, about 7-8 hours away from Downtown Toronto. Every students in our school are required to visit this island during the fall of grade 9 and again in the winter of grade 10 and we refer to these trips as Fall Temagami and Winter Temagami.

Fall Temagami

This trip happened right at the beginning of the school year and it is a canoe trip. We started with a long bus ride, then they dropped us off, and we got on a boat to get to the main campus on the Island where we learn some basic survival skills and how to paddle a canoe even though most of my tripmates already knew how because Canadians love camping. Then in the following days, it was all about canoeing to different sites, setting up our tents, chopping woods, and cooking meals. At night, we would sit around the campfire, chat, play card games (since the teachers took away out phones), and gaze at the stars.

Winter Temagami

I just want to say that I love this trip and I enjoyed this adventure a lot. When we got to the drop-off point, we had to trek 10 km to get to the island because the lack was completely frozen. It took us 3 hours to get there because the snow were THICK and very difficult to walk on. Our guides then picked a site on top of the frozen lake buried under layers of snow, where we built the quinzhees , which is a snow shelter made from a large pile of loose snow. We stayed there for a couple days and I still remember my tripmates and I were freezing all the time since Canadian winter's is super super cold plus there is no buildings or skyscrapers to block the wind. So just like Fall Temagami, we spend our nights gathering around the campfire to eat, chatting, playing games, and basically doing everything around it.
